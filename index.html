Using Computer Vision to Analyze Bioluminescent Displays

By Raj Saha, PhD

Light is probably the most common language of communication in the animal kingdom and we are just beginning to understand how animals use light to communicate with each other. Most of the space occupied by organisms on the planet is dark, and that's where bioluminescence has evolved as a tool for survival and communication.

In this project we use computer vision to track and analyze individual fireflies from field recordings. The goal is to understand the pulse characteristics of different fireflies and use mathematical models to simulate their collective behaviors of synchrony. Several species of fireflies, and possibly marine bioluminescent organisms, are known to synchronize their light displays, possibly to attract mates or as a defense mechanisms. In a collaboration with Dr. Trevor Rivers of University of Kansas, we developed a computer vision algorithm to track the movement of individual fireflies and extract their pulse characteristics to use in mathematical models.
